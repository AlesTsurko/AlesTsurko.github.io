Concept: Almira Ousmanova
Artists: Natalia Nenarokomova, Ales Potapenko
Sound: Ales Tsurko

To increasingly rapid acceleration of cinema you can respond only by its slowing. The installation actualizes concepts of time and space in the Tarkovsky's Solaris. It’s a possibility to dive into the film experience as a sufficient and finished audiovisual world at the moment of estrangement from a cinematic narrative.

Through digital hyperbolization of time and space to 24 hours the sound effect of each pixel that finds a combination with a soundtrack is achieved.

There are two videos on two screens. The first video starts from zoom-in and scaled through zoom-out, and the second one has a reverse action. This effect allows the audience to dive into picture, to become a part of it, to become a place where Tarkovsky’s cinematic images are connected.

The idea of the sound is in immersion to each frame.

The immersion occurs to the pixel level, the quantum of digital image. As well as in the original soundtrack that Eduard Artemyev has wrote on the ANS, there is a transformation of visible into audible in the 24h Solaris. But here is a different technique - the sonification of main colors of the each frame. The interaction of colors becomes not only a harmony technique, but also the rhythm, dynamic and articulation. The Solaris itself becomes a musical form.

The average color tone used as well as the pixels separately. The tone is transitions between frames and dynamic of action. Harmony also appears at a deeper level: the pixel color is a mixture of red, green and blue, each of which corresponds to its own timbre.

The original soundtrack as material for sound wave synthesis. This is done to reflect dynamic of the Solaris’ sound and for getting a deeper texture.

A program that is written in Processing slows down the film to 12% as well as in the projections. Then it analyze the result. Each frame split symmetrically by 24 points: 8 horizontal and 3 vertical. The color value of each point transferred via OSC to SuperCollider.

The program sends data with intervals. It’s like a screenshot every 4 seconds. The length of the interval defines a tempo and the texture richness.

8-bit digital color has a range from 0 to 255. For the red, for example, the range have mapped to 0-17 that corresponds to 6 notes of the pitch set in three octaves.

The average value is the mood of the frame. For this reason the range of this value also mapped to pitch set. The pitch is added to the pixels stream and defines the tonic, scale and mood. The pitch set has composed to make changes in the music mood, when the mood of image changes.

The color ranges also mapped to dynamic, articulation and rhythm. And as the main mood at the moment, the average value is a multiplier of parameters.

A rhythm figure of some phrases programmed to fit 4 seconds. But other doesn’t get new data while phrase is playing. This shadows result to smooth form and quit flow.

Texture includes seven instruments: a texture red, green, blue; a percussion red, green, blue; a “lead” that synthesized from the original soundtrack.

The lead plays the average value of a RGB sum.

The program works in real time.
