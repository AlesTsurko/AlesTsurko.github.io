Aleś Curko (the full name is Aliaksandr Uladzimiravich Tsurko) is a belarusian self-taught composer and software engineer. He was born on March 16, 1987 in Minsk. He graduated from the accordion class of a music school in 2000 and a percussion class in 2005. Aleś had composed music from early childhood, but started to write electronic music since 15, when he got a computer.

In 2011 he founded the Society For The Development Of New Music, the main purpose of which had been to develop and promote contemporary classical music. The members were composers and teachers of the Belarusian State Academy of Music: Kanstantsin Yaskou, Maksim Kruhly and Olga Podhayskaya. He also founded the Belarus Modern Ensemble and initiated the Belarus Modern Orchestra, both of which were specialized in contemporary classical music. Between 2012 and 2014 he was working as a sound engineer at an independent recording studio.

Aleś is using software written by himself and combining electronic and electroacoustic with contemporary classical and rock music. His activity isn't only the music, but also interactive art and new media forms.
